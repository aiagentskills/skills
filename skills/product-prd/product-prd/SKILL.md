# Product PRD

## Goal

Turn discovery notes into a structured PRD with clear goals, scope, requirements, and success metrics.

## When to use

- Write a PRD for a feature
- Turn notes into a structured PRD
- Define scope and success metrics

## Inputs to request

- Problem statement and target users
- Goals and non-goals
- Requirements (functional and non-functional)
- Constraints, dependencies, and risks
- Milestones and timeline
- Success metrics and launch criteria

## Workflow

1. Restate the problem and audience.
2. Define goals, non-goals, and scope.
3. Capture requirements and acceptance criteria.
4. Identify dependencies and risks.
5. Outline milestones and rollout.
6. Define success metrics and launch criteria.
7. List open questions.

## Output structure

- Summary
- Goals and non-goals
- Scope
- Requirements
- Milestones and rollout
- Risks and dependencies
- Success metrics
- Open questions
